Mulligatawny soup Recipe

History

Mulligatawny soup has its origins in South India, where it began as a spiced lentil-based dish known as "molo tunny," which means "pepper water" in Tamil. This dish was adapted by the British during the colonial period to suit European palates, incorporating meat, vegetables, and creamy elements. Over time, it evolved into a fusion dish, blending Indian spices with European culinary techniques.

Ingredients

    For 4 servings:

    Vegetables and Base:

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or ghee
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated

    • Spices:

  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)

    • Other Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup red lentils (or yellow split peas)
  • 1 apple, peeled and diced (adds a sweet-tangy flavor)
  • 4 cups chicken or vegetable stock
  • 1 cup cooked chicken (optional, for non-vegetarian versions)
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk or heavy cream
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Salt and pepper to taste

    • Garnish:

  • Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped
  • A dollop of yogurt or sour cream (optional)

Procedure

    repare the Base:

  1. Heat the oil or ghee in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, and celery, and sauté until softened (about 5–7 minutes).
  2. Add the garlic and ginger, and cook for another minute until fragrant.

    3. Add Spices:

  3. Stir in the turmeric, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, and cayenne pepper. Cook for 1–2 minutes to toast the spices and enhance their flavor.

    4. Incorporate Lentils and Apple:

  4. Add the lentils and diced apple to the pot. Stir well to coat them with the spices.

    5. Simmer the Soup:

  5. Pour in the stock and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 20–25 minutes, or until the lentils are tender.

    6. Blend (Optional):

  6. Use an immersion blender to partially blend the soup for a thicker consistency. If you prefer a chunkier texture, skip this step.

    7. Add Protein and Creaminess:

  7. Stir in the cooked chicken (if using), coconut milk or cream, and lemon juice. Simmer for another 5 minutes to heat through.

    8. Season and Serve:

  8. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper. Ladle the soup into bowls, garnish with cilantro and a dollop of yogurt or sour cream, and serve hot.
