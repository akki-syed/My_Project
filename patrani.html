patrani machhi Recipe

History

Patrani Macchi is a classic Parsi dish made from fish, usually steamed and marinated in a fragrant blend of spices, wrapped in banana leaves, and then cooked to perfection. The name Patrani Macchi literally translates to "fish in leaves," referring to the method of cooking, where the fish is wrapped in banana leaves to retain moisture and infuse the flavors of the marinade.Patrani Macchi has its roots in Parsi cuisine, a unique blend of Persian and Indian flavors. The Parsis, who migrated to India from Persia (modern-day Iran) centuries ago, brought with them various culinary traditions that evolved and adapted to local ingredients and tastes. Patrani Macchi is one of the most iconic and beloved dishes in Parsi homes, particularly during festive occasions and celebrations. The use of banana leaves for steaming fish is a traditional cooking technique found across different cultures in Asia and the Indian subcontinent, and in Parsi cuisine, it imparts a distinct aroma and flavor to the dish. Patrani Macchi has become an essential part of Parsi weddings, festivals, and special gatherings.

Ingredients

    For the Fish Marinade:

  • 4-5 medium-sized fish fillets (pomfret, kingfish, or any firm white fish is preferred)
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro (coriander) leaves, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, finely chopped
  • 2-3 green chilies, chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind pulp or vinegar
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional, balances acidity)
  • 2 tablespoons fresh grated coconut (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil or ghee

    • For Wrapping:

  • Banana leaves (enough to wrap the fish fillets)
  • Ghee or oil for greasing the banana leaves

Procedure

    Step 1: Prepare the Fish

  1. Clean and wash the fish fillets thoroughly. Pat them dry with a paper towel.
  2. Make a few shallow cuts on both sides of the fish fillets to help the marinade penetrate better.

    3. Step 2: Make the Marinade

  3. In a bowl, combine the chopped cilantro, mint, green chilies, ginger-garlic paste, coriander powder, cumin powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, lime juice, tamarind pulp or vinegar, salt, sugar (if using), and grated coconut (if using).
  4. Add the oil or ghee to the mixture and mix well until you get a smooth paste.
  5. Coat the fish fillets with the marinade thoroughly, making sure that the marinade is applied on both sides of the fish and inside the cuts.
  6. Let the fish marinate for at least 30 minutes, but preferably for 2-3 hours (or overnight in the refrigerator) for the flavors to develop.

    7. Step 3: Prepare the Banana Leaves

  7. Cut the banana leaves into squares or large enough pieces to wrap the fish fillets comfortably.
  8. If the banana leaves are stiff, you can gently heat them over an open flame or dip them in hot water for a few seconds to make them more pliable and easier to fold.
  9. Lightly grease the banana leaves with some oil or ghee to prevent sticking and to enhance the flavor.

    10. Step 4: Wrap the Fish

  10. Place one marinated fish fillet in the center of a banana leaf piece.
  11. Fold the sides of the banana leaf over the fish, covering it completely. Secure the packet by folding the edges or tying it with a string or another piece of leaf.
  12. Repeat with the remaining fish fillets.

    13. Step 5: Steam the Fish

  13. Prepare a steamer by filling a pot with water and bringing it to a simmer. Alternatively, you can use a pressure cooker (without the weight) to steam the fish.
  14. Place the banana leaf-wrapped fish parcels in the steamer basket, ensuring that they do not touch the water directly.
  15. Cover the steamer and cook the fish for about 20-30 minutes, or until the fish is tender and cooked through. You can check by gently opening one parcel and ensuring the fish is opaque and flakes easily with a fork.

    16. Step 6: Serve

  16. Carefully remove the parcels from the steamer.Serve the Patrani Macchi hot, garnished with some extra fresh herbs or lime wedges if desired.

    17. Patrani Macchi is best enjoyed with steamed rice or Indian flatbreads like naan or paratha.
