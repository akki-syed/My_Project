aviyal Recipe

History

Aviyal has its origins in Kerala, and it is considered one of the oldest dishes in South India, dating back to ancient times. The dish is mentioned in the Mahabharata, an epic from Hindu mythology, where it is said that Aviyal was prepared for Lord Krishna. The word "Aviyal" means "mix" or "mixture," which refers to the combination of various vegetables in the dish. It is traditionally believed to have been prepared by the Tamil kings during festivals as part of a vegetarian feast. Over time, Aviyal became a staple dish in Kerala's sadya (a traditional multi-course meal), served during special occasions, and it spread to neighboring states like Tamil Nadu.

Ingredients

  • Vegetables: Typically, a combination of drumstick (moringa), carrots, beans, raw banana, potatoes, pumpkin, and cucumber. The variety can vary based on availability.
  • Coconut: Grated fresh coconut, which forms the base of the paste.Spices: Green chilies, turmeric powder, and cumin seeds.Curd (yogurt): Adds a tangy flavor to the dish.
  • Curry leaves: To enhance the flavor.
  • Coconut oil: Traditional oil used for seasoning.
  • Salt: To taste.

Procedure

    Preparation of Vegetables:

  1. Peel and cut the vegetables into medium-sized pieces. Make sure the vegetables are of similar size to ensure even cooking.

    2. Grinding Coconut Paste:

  2. In a blender, grind grated coconut, green chilies, cumin seeds, and turmeric powder into a smooth paste, adding a little water if necessary.

    3. Cooking the Vegetables:

  3. In a large pan or pot, add the hard vegetables first, like carrots and potatoes. Cook them in some water until they are slightly tender.
  4. Add the remaining vegetables, including raw banana, pumpkin, and beans. Add enough water to cook the vegetables, and a pinch of salt.
  5. Cook on a medium flame until all the vegetables are tender but still firm.

    6. Adding Coconut Paste:

  6. Once the vegetables are cooked, add the coconut paste to the pot and mix gently to combine. Allow it to cook for about 5-10 minutes on low heat.

    7. Adding Yogurt and Seasoning:

  7. Reduce the heat and add yogurt to the mixture. Stir well to prevent curdling. You can adjust the yogurt according to the level of tanginess you prefer.
  8. Add a few curry leaves and a drizzle of coconut oil. Let it simmer for a few minutes, ensuring all the flavors are well incorporated.

    9. Finishing:

  9. Taste and adjust salt or yogurt as needed. The dish should have a slightly creamy, thick consistency.

    10. Serving:

  10. Serve hot with steamed rice, as part of a sadya or a simple meal.
