Pork Sorpotel Recipe

History

Pork Sorpotel is a traditional Goan dish, known for its rich, spicy, and tangy flavors. It’s a popular dish in Portuguese-influenced Indian cuisine, especially in Goa, and is often served during special occasions and festive celebrations. The dish is made with pork, marinated in a special blend of spices, and simmered with vinegar, making it tender and flavorful. The dish has its roots in Portuguese colonial influence on India, particularly Goa, where Portuguese missionaries and settlers introduced many European culinary traditions. The word "sorpotel" itself is derived from the Portuguese word "sarapatel," which refers to a similar dish made with offal and meat. Over time, Goan cooks adapted this dish to local tastes, substituting pork for offal and adding more indigenous spices. Sorpotel became a staple in Goan Catholic households and is often served with "sannas" (a type of fluffy steamed bread).

Ingredients

  • Pork (boneless, cut into cubes): 500g (you can use pork belly, shoulder, or a combination)
  • Pork liver (optional): 100g (for an authentic version, but can be omitted)
  • Onions: 2 medium-sized, finely chopped
  • Tomatoes: 2, chopped
  • Garlic cloves: 6-8, minced
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece, minced
  • Green chilies: 2-3, slit
  • Vinegar: 3-4 tbsp (preferably malt or coconut vinegar)
  • Tamarind paste: 1 tbsp (optional, for additional tanginess)
  • Cinnamon stick: 1
  • Cloves: 4-5
  • Cardamom pods: 2
  • Cumin seeds: 1 tsp
  • Mustard seeds: 1 tsp
  • Peppercorns: 1 tsp
  • Turmeric powder: ½ tspv
  • Red chili powder: 1 tsp
  • Garam masala: 1 tsp
  • Sugar: 1 tsp (optional, to balance the tanginess)
  • Salt: To taste
  • Oil: 2 tbsp (for cooking)

Procedure

    Prepare the Meat:

  1. If using pork liver, cut it into small pieces and set aside. Clean the pork and cut it into small cubes. You can marinate the pork with a bit of vinegar, salt, and turmeric for 30 minutes for enhanced flavor.

    2. Make the Spice Paste:In a small pan, dry roast the cumin seeds, mustard seeds, cloves, cinnamon stick, cardamom, and peppercorns until aromatic. Let them cool and grind them into a fine powder along with the red chili powder, turmeric, and garam masala.

    Cook the Pork:

  2. Heat oil in a large pan and sauté the onions until golden brown. Add the garlic, ginger, and green chilies, and sauté for another minute.
  3. Add the tomatoes and cook until soft and pulpy. Then add the ground spice paste and cook until the oil begins to separate from the masala.

    4. Add the Meat:

  4. Add the pork cubes (and liver, if using) to the pan and cook on medium-high heat, stirring occasionally, until the meat starts to brown.

    5. Simmer:

  5. Add the vinegar, tamarind paste (if using), sugar, and salt. Pour enough water to cover the meat and bring it to a boil. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 1-1.5 hours, or until the meat is tender and the sauce has thickened. Stir occasionally, and add more water if needed to keep the consistency as per your preference.

    6. Final Adjustments:Taste the sorpotel and adjust the seasoning, adding more vinegar, salt, or sugar as desired. It’s common to let the sorpotel sit overnight for the flavors to develop more intensely.

    Serve:

  6. Pork Sorpotel is traditionally served with "sannas" (Goan rice bread) or plain rice, but it can also be paired with other Indian flatbreads.

    7. Enjoy this delicious and spicy Goan delicacy!
