Yakhni Pulao Recipe

History

Yakhni Pulao is a fragrant rice dish rooted in the culinary traditions of Central and South Asia. Its origins trace back to the Mughal era, where it was developed as a sophisticated yet hearty meal for royalty. The word "Yakhni" refers to a flavorful broth made by simmering meat with aromatic spices, which forms the foundation of the dish. Influenced by Persian and Central Asian cuisines, Yakhni Pulao traveled to different regions, evolving into a beloved dish in Indian, Pakistani, and Afghan kitchens.

Ingredients

    For the Yakhni (Broth):

  • 500g meat (chicken, mutton, or beef)
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 4-5 garlic cloves
  • 1-inch ginger piece (sliced)
  • 4-5 whole black peppercorns
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • 2-3 green cardamoms
  • 1 black cardamom
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds
  • 6 cups water
  • Salt to taste

    • For the Pulao:

  • 2 cups basmati rice (soaked for 30 minutes)
  • 3 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1 onion (finely sliced)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 2 green cardamoms
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 2-3 tbsp yogurt (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves for garnish

Procedure

    Step 1: Preparing the Yakhni (Broth):

  1. Place the meat in a large pot and add the water, onions, garlic, ginger, and all the whole spices.
  2. Simmer on low heat for about 45 minutes (or until the meat is tender).
  3. Strain the broth into a bowl, separating the meat and discarding the spices and residue. Keep the meat aside.

    4. Step 2: Cooking the Pulao:

  4. Heat ghee or oil in a heavy-bottomed pot. Add cumin seeds, cloves, cardamoms, and cinnamon stick. SautÃ© for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add the sliced onions and cook until golden brown.
  6. Stir in the yogurt (if using) and green chilies. Cook for 1-2 minutes.
  7. Add the cooked meat pieces and sautÃ© for a few minutes to coat them with the spices.
  8. Add the strained Yakhni (broth) to the pot. For 2 cups of rice, youâ€™ll need about 4 cups of liquid. Adjust salt as needed.
  9. Once the broth starts boiling, gently add the soaked and drained basmati rice.
  10. Cover the pot and cook on low heat for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and fluffy.
  11. Let it rest for 5 minutes, then fluff the rice with a fork.

    12. Step 3: Garnishing and Serving:

  12. Garnish with fresh coriander and mint leaves before serving.
  13. Yakhni Pulao is best enjoyed with a side of raita, salad, or pickles.

    14. Notes

  14. You can enhance the flavor by adding fried nuts or raisins during garnishing.
  15. For an extra layer of aroma, sprinkle a few drops of rose water or kewra essence before serving.
